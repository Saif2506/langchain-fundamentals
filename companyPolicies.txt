Innovatech Company Policies

Policy 1: Mobile Phone Usage
Employees are permitted to use personal mobile phones during work hours for brief personal communications. However, excessive use of mobile phones during meetings, training sessions, or while operating machinery is strictly prohibited. Employees must set their phones to silent or vibrate mode while in open office areas to minimize disruption to colleagues. The use of mobile phones for photography or video recording within secure areas of the facility is not allowed without prior written authorization from the Security Department. Employees who violate this policy may face disciplinary action, including verbal warnings for first offenses and written warnings for repeated violations. Company-issued mobile devices must be used primarily for business purposes and returned upon termination of employment.

Policy 2: Smoking Policy
Innovatech is committed to providing a healthy and comfortable working environment for all employees. Smoking, including the use of electronic cigarettes and vaping devices, is strictly prohibited inside all company buildings, including offices, restrooms, hallways, and stairwells. Designated smoking areas have been established at least 25 feet from building entrances, exits, and air intake vents. Employees must use these designated areas during approved break times only. Cigarette butts and related waste must be disposed of in the provided receptacles. Employees who smoke are encouraged to participate in the company's smoking cessation program, which is available free of charge through the Employee Wellness Center. Violations of this policy will result in progressive disciplinary action.

Policy 3: Vehicle Policy
All employees who operate company vehicles must possess a valid driver's license appropriate for the type of vehicle being driven. Company vehicles are to be used exclusively for business-related purposes unless otherwise authorized by management. Employees must conduct a pre-trip inspection of the vehicle before each use, checking tires, lights, fluid levels, and overall vehicle condition. Any accidents, damage, or mechanical issues must be reported to the Fleet Manager immediately. Personal use of company vehicles is not permitted without prior written approval. Employees are responsible for any traffic violations incurred while operating a company vehicle. Seatbelts must be worn at all times, and the use of mobile phones while driving is strictly prohibited. Company vehicles must be returned clean and with at least a quarter tank of fuel.

Policy 4: Internet and Email Usage
The internet and email systems provided by Innovatech are primarily for business use. Limited personal use is permitted during break times, provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities or violate any other company policies. Employees must not use company internet or email to access, download, or distribute inappropriate, offensive, or illegal content. All emails sent through the company system should maintain a professional tone and comply with confidentiality requirements. Employees should be aware that the company reserves the right to monitor internet usage and email communications in accordance with applicable laws. Downloading unauthorized software, streaming entertainment content during work hours, and using peer-to-peer file sharing applications are strictly prohibited. Employees must report any suspicious emails or potential phishing attempts to the IT Security team immediately.

Policy 5: Dress Code Policy
Innovatech maintains a business casual dress code to promote a professional yet comfortable work environment. Employees are expected to present a neat, clean, and professional appearance at all times. Business casual attire includes collared shirts, blouses, slacks, khakis, skirts of appropriate length, and closed-toe shoes. Jeans are permitted on Fridays, provided they are clean and free of rips or tears. Employees who interact with clients or attend formal meetings are expected to dress in business professional attire. T-shirts with offensive graphics or messages, flip-flops, shorts, and athletic wear are not considered appropriate workplace attire. Employees working in laboratory or manufacturing areas must comply with specific safety dress requirements, including wearing appropriate personal protective equipment. Managers have the discretion to address dress code violations on a case-by-case basis.

Policy 6: Remote Work Policy
Eligible employees may request to work remotely up to two days per week, subject to manager approval. Remote work arrangements must not negatively impact team collaboration, productivity, or the ability to meet job responsibilities. Employees working remotely must maintain a dedicated workspace that is safe, secure, and free from distractions. All company data accessed remotely must be handled in accordance with the Information Security Policy, including the use of VPN connections and encrypted devices. Remote employees are expected to be available during core business hours (9:00 AM to 3:00 PM) and must respond to communications in a timely manner. Employees must track their work hours accurately and submit timesheets as required. The company reserves the right to modify or revoke remote work privileges at any time based on business needs or performance concerns. Equipment provided by the company for remote work must be returned upon termination of the remote work arrangement or employment.

Policy 7: Workplace Safety Policy
Innovatech is committed to maintaining a safe and healthy workplace for all employees, visitors, and contractors. All employees are responsible for following established safety procedures, reporting hazards, and participating in required safety training programs. Employees must immediately report any workplace injuries, accidents, or near-miss incidents to their supervisor and the Safety Department. The use of personal protective equipment is mandatory in designated areas, and employees must not tamper with or disable any safety devices or equipment. Emergency exits and evacuation routes must be kept clear at all times. Fire drills and emergency evacuation exercises will be conducted quarterly. Employees found violating safety policies may face disciplinary action up to and including termination. The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for workplace violence, threats, or intimidation.

Policy 8: Confidentiality and Data Protection Policy
All employees are required to protect the confidentiality of company information, trade secrets, and proprietary data. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to, financial data, customer lists, product designs, marketing strategies, and employee records. Employees must not disclose confidential information to unauthorized individuals, whether inside or outside the company. All confidential documents must be stored securely, and electronic files must be protected with appropriate access controls and passwords. Employees must shred or securely dispose of documents containing sensitive information when they are no longer needed. The use of personal email accounts or unauthorized cloud storage services to transmit or store company data is strictly prohibited. Employees who leave the company must return all confidential materials and certify that they have not retained any copies. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination, and potential legal proceedings.

Policy 9: Leave and Attendance Policy
Regular attendance is essential for the smooth operation of Innovatech. Employees are expected to report to work on time and adhere to their scheduled work hours. If an employee is unable to report to work or will be late, they must notify their supervisor as soon as possible, preferably before the start of their shift. Innovatech provides various types of leave, including annual leave, sick leave, personal leave, and parental leave, in accordance with applicable laws and company policy. Employees must submit leave requests through the HR portal at least two weeks in advance for planned absences. Sick leave of three or more consecutive days may require a medical certificate upon return to work. Excessive unexcused absences or a pattern of absenteeism may result in disciplinary action. Employees are encouraged to review the full Leave Policy document available on the company intranet for detailed information about leave entitlements and procedures.
